Dj Party


DJ Party is a web application I developed for my Advanced Web Design Studio course at Columbia University. The idea was to create an application to help a DJ to manage song requests made by people at a party and then improve his playlist based on a recommendation report generated at the end of the party. The front-end of the application was developed using HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, jQuery and the back-end was a Python Flask Api with a SQL database.


I decided to use a black and white color theme for the application. The button's are highlighted with black to guide the user through the application.


I used images related to popular dj's around the world to set the tone and vibe of the application. I placed the text on the empty spaces of the background images so that the space is efficiently utilised on the page while still drawing the attention of the user to the text.


I used color contrast to highlight the text that was more relevant to user (such as song names) while providing other information along with it (such as artist names) in a lighter shade.


I also developed a search functionality to assist user's in finding songs on the application. The application also included a navigation bar at the top to help user's to easily traverse through the different pages.

Abs Blaster


For the User Interface Design course at Columbia University I developed a fitness application for users who want to work specifically on their ab muscles. The goal of the application was to help a user plan and create an abs workout specific to their own body type. The users were given the ability to customise their workout for the level of difficulty that they wanted to achieve. Once the user had set their goals they could then workout along with the videos provided.


For curating their workout I provided a check box to the user's so that they can easily select/unselect exercise videos that they wanted to add/remove from their workout session. Each exercise had a small video associated with it to help user's understand the exercise they were picking for their workout.


The user was also given a drag and drop functionality to inter-change the order of the exercises in their planned workout session.


I used bold/normal text to highlight the important text on the pages. I also used white spaces to make the application less cluttered and allow user's to concentrate on their main goal of planing a custom workout.


The user was given an ability to easily navigate through pages and an option to go back at any time to modify their workout. The buttons were highlighted with a black color on the white background to help user's easily navigate through the application and direct them to expected next step in the application.

Mumbai Travel


In order to learn React I created a web app for a travel guide to the city of Mumbai. It included recommendations for all the best places to visit, food to eat, hidden gems for shopping and some of the best festivals to look out for in Mumbai.


I used images to enhance the beauty of the city and help users's to navigate to specific pages containing detailed information about the must visit spots.


I used white spaces and color contrast to highlight the important text. I also provided a navigation bar to help them easily access the different pages.


Apart from that I used the google maps api to help user's see the different spots on the map of Mumbai. This would help the user to create a better travel plan while keeping in mind the distance between the various places they would like to visit.

We Hire


As one of my assignments in the Advanced Web Design Studio class. I also created a website to help students and recruiters connect at Career fairs. The main goal of the application was to provide a platform to both parties to effortlessly communicate announcements and/or queries with one another.



The recruiters could see the list of all the students registered for the event and vice versa. They could also view all the messages sent by them on their own profile. I used jQuery and socket.io to provide seamless communication between multiple users.



The site allowed users to reply to specific messages in real-time. It showed the most recent messages at the top to ensure that both students and recruiters are viewing the latest information at all times.
